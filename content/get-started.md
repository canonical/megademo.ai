# Get started

Madrid Engineering Sprint AI hackathon. See the announcement on Discourse: [Initialize engineering sprint MegaDemo.ai 👾 hackathon](https://discourse.canonical.com/t/initialize-engineering-sprint-megademo-ai-hackathon/7305).

## Sign in

Click **Sign in with GitHub** — you need a `@canonical.com` GitHub account.
That's it. No separate registration.

---

## Browse projects

**[Projects](/projects)** are public — no sign-in needed.
Sort by newest or top-rated. Click any card to see full details, demo videos, and repo links.

---

## Register a project

1. Sign in, then click **+ Register Project**.
2. Pick a **category** (Coding Assistant, Automation, Data & Analytics, etc. — or "Other").
3. Fill in title, short description, and select your **Canonical team**.
4. Tag the **AI tools** and **tech stack** you used (you can add custom).
5. (Optional) Add repo link(s) and Asciinema/YouTube/Vimeo demo URL.
6. Submit — your project is live immediately.

> **Tip:** You can edit everything after submitting.

---

## Add a demo

On your project page, upload an **Asciinema** cast or paste a **YouTube/Vimeo** URL.
Videos appear embedded on the project page.

---

## Join a teammate's project

Open the project page → click **Join Project**.
Joining grants edit access — it's trust-based. Be nice!
You can leave at any time.

---

## Vote

Browse projects and use the **★ star rating** (1–5) on any project page.
You can change your vote. You cannot vote on your own project.

---

## Submission due time

The countdown on the homepage shows time remaining.
When the submission time is up, new registrations close, and the leaderboard locks.
The second countdown then shows time until the live **MegaDemo** presentation.
